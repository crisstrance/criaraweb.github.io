Villa de Pedraza is my final project for a Degree in Graphic Design at ArteDiez Art School (Madrid). The main objective was to redesign the tourist brochure and map of Pedraza, a small medieval town in Segovia. Declared a historic site by UNESCO in 1951, Pedraza has remained largely intact over time but was in need of a refreshed visual identity.

To achieve this, the project include:
¬∑ A compact logo and a simplified town shield, giving it a modern touch.
¬∑ A tourist map highlighting key points of interest.
¬∑ Two brochures, one of which focuses on Pedraza as a popular filming location.
¬∑ Some merchandising items, including postcards, bags, t-shirts, travel journals, and mugs.
The project aimed to combine heritage preservation with contemporary design, making Pedraza's charm accessible and appealing to modern visitors.
